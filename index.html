01 — Profile

Migration,
Media &
Power

I am a political scientist whose work sits at the crossroads of political communication, migration studies, and gender politics. My research examines how media constructs and circulates narratives about migrants and refugees — particularly how visual and discursive framing strategies serve political ends during electoral cycles and authoritarian consolidation.

My methodological toolkit draws from quantitative media analysis, survey design, gravity modelling, and visual framing theory. I am equally at home with large-scale datasets and ethnographically grounded field research.

Currently a Lecturer at Eskişehir Osmangazi University, where I have taught since 2018. I completed a TÜBİTAK-funded Visiting Research Fellowship at the University of Sussex (2024–2025).
